Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best bank account for college students?

Chase College Checking is the best overall â€” $0 fees for ages 17-24, 15,000+ ATMs, $100 sign-up bonus, and Zelle for splitting bills with roommates. For online-only with rewards, Discover Cashback Debit offers 1% cashback on all debit purchases with no fees ever.

Do student bank accounts have monthly fees?

Most student-specific accounts charge $0 monthly fees while you're enrolled (typically until age 24-25). After that, some convert to standard accounts with $5-12/month fees. Online banks like Discover, Ally, and Capital One 360 never charge fees regardless of student status.

Can I open a student bank account at 17?

Yes. Chase College Checking accepts ages 17+. Bank of America Advantage SafePass accepts ages 16+. Wells Fargo Clear Access accepts ages 13+ (with parent co-owner until 18). Most other banks require you to be 18.

Should I use a big bank or online bank as a student?

If you need to deposit cash or want in-person help, choose a big bank (Chase, BofA). If you do everything digitally and want higher interest rates and cashback, go online (Discover, Ally, SoFi). Many students use one of each â€” a big bank for cash deposits and an online bank for savings.

What happens to my student bank account after I graduate?

Most accounts convert to standard checking with potential monthly fees ($5-12). Chase converts at age 24 ($12/month, waivable). BofA converts after 25 ($4.95/month). Online banks (Discover, Ally, Capital One 360, SoFi) stay free forever â€” switch before your student account expires to avoid fees.

Do student bank accounts affect my credit score?

No. Opening a checking or savings account doesn't affect your credit score. Banks may run a ChexSystems check (not a credit check). However, unpaid overdrafts sent to collections will hurt your credit. To build credit, pair your bank account with a student or secured credit card.

How much money do I need to open a student bank account?

Most require $0-$25. Chase, Discover, Capital One 360, Ally, SoFi, and Chime all require $0. Bank of America and Wells Fargo require $25. There are no ongoing minimum balance requirements for most student accounts.
