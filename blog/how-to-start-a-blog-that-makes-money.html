How to Validate Your Niche

Before committing, check three things:

  1. Search demand: Use Google Trends and free keyword tools (Ubersuggest, Google Keyword Planner) to confirm people are searching for your topic
  2. Monetization potential: Search "[your niche] affiliate programs" ‚Äî if there are multiple programs paying $20+ per conversion, the niche is monetizable
  3. Competition level: Search your main keywords on Google. If page 1 is dominated by massive brands (Forbes, NerdWallet, WebMD), look for a more specific sub-niche. If you see independent blogs ranking, there's room for you.

Passion vs. Profit ‚Äî Finding the Sweet Spot

The ideal niche sits at the intersection of three circles:

  • What you know about ‚Äî expertise or experience (even as a beginner learning publicly)
  • What people search for ‚Äî proven demand via keyword research
  • What pays well ‚Äî affiliate programs, ad revenue, product potential

Don't pick a niche purely for money if you have zero interest ‚Äî you'll burn out before it becomes profitable. And don't pick a passion project with zero search demand ‚Äî no one will find your content.

Step 2: Pick Your Blog Name & Domain

Domain Name Tips & Tools

  • Keep it short: 2‚Äì3 words is ideal. Easy to type, easy to remember.
  • Make it brandable: "BudgetSmartLiving" is better than "BestBudgetingTips2026"
  • Avoid hyphens and numbers: They look spammy and are hard to share verbally

.com vs. Other Extensions

Always try for a .com domain first. It's the most trusted and recognizable extension. If your exact .com is taken, try adding a word (e.g., "the," "my," "get") rather than switching to .net or .co. A .com domain is included free for the first year with Bluehost.

Step 5: Write Your First 10 Blog Posts

Content Strategy for New Blogs (Pillar + Supporting Posts)

Don't just write random posts. Use the pillar + cluster model:

  • Pillar posts (3‚Äì4): Comprehensive, 3,000‚Äì5,000-word guides targeting your main keywords. These are the "hub" articles that rank for competitive terms.
  • Supporting posts (6‚Äì7): Shorter, 1,500‚Äì2,500-word articles targeting related long-tail keywords. These link back to your pillar posts, boosting their authority.

Example for a personal finance blog:

  • Pillar: "How to Save Money on Groceries" (comprehensive guide)
  • Supporting: "10 Cheap Meal Prep Ideas," "Best Coupon Apps," "How to Plan a Weekly Meal Budget"

SEO Basics Every New Blogger Needs to Know

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is how Google finds and ranks your content. You don't need to be an expert, but these basics are essential:

  1. Keyword research: Use free tools (Google Keyword Planner, Ubersuggest, AnswerThePublic) to find what people search for in your niche
  2. Target one primary keyword per post: Include it in your title, URL, first paragraph, and 2‚Äì3 headings
  3. Write for humans first, search engines second: Google rewards helpful, comprehensive content that answers the searcher's question
  4. Use headers (H2, H3) to organize: Break up your content into scannable sections
  5. Internal link between your posts: Every new post should link to 2‚Äì3 related posts on your blog
  6. Install Yoast SEO or Rank Math: Free WordPress plugins that guide you through on-page optimization

How to Write Posts That Rank on Google

The formula for ranking blog posts:

  1. Search your keyword on Google and study the top 5 results. What do they cover? How long are they?
  2. Create something better: More comprehensive, more up-to-date, better organized, with unique insights or data
  3. Match search intent: If people searching "best budgeting apps" expect a comparison list, don't write an essay about budgeting philosophy
  4. Make it scannable: Short paragraphs, bullet points, comparison tables, bold key phrases
  5. Add value others don't: Original screenshots, real-world examples, personal experience, unique data
