The 7 Best Money Transfer Apps (Detailed Reviews)

1. Zelle ‚Äî Best for Free Domestic Transfers

Fees: $0 ‚Äî completely free
Speed: Minutes (typically under 5 minutes)
Daily limit: $500-$5,000 (varies by bank)
International: Not available
Available through: 2,000+ bank and credit union apps

Zelle is the simplest, cheapest way to send money to anyone in the U.S. It's built directly into most major bank apps ‚Äî Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Capital One, US Bank, and over 2,000 others ‚Äî so there's nothing new to download or sign up for. Money moves directly from your bank account to theirs in minutes with zero fees.

Why Zelle wins for domestic transfers:

  • Zero fees, ever ‚Äî No transaction fees, no monthly fees, no instant transfer fees
  • Bank-level security ‚Äî Runs through your bank's existing security infrastructure (encryption, authentication, fraud monitoring)
  • No app needed ‚Äî Already built into your banking app; just look for the Zelle icon
  • Send with just email or phone number ‚Äî No need to exchange account numbers
  • No balance to maintain ‚Äî Money moves directly between bank accounts; no intermediate wallet to manage

Real example: Four roommates split $2,400 in monthly rent and utilities. Using Zelle, each person sends their share ($600) to whoever pays the landlord. That's 48 transfers per year at $0 each. Using Venmo instant transfer instead would cost each person $9/month ($108/year per person).

Watch out for: Zelle transfers are instant and irreversible. If you send money to the wrong person or get scammed, your bank generally cannot reverse the transaction. Only use Zelle with people you know and trust. For purchasing goods from strangers, use PayPal Goods & Services instead.

Security Tips for Safe Money Transfers

Protect yourself when sending money digitally:

  1. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) ‚Äî Every money transfer app offers this; turn it on immediately
  2. Use biometrics ‚Äî Enable Face ID or fingerprint unlock for your payment apps
  3. Verify recipient details ‚Äî Double-check the email, phone number, or account number before sending. With Zelle and bank transfers, mistakes are irreversible
  4. Never send money to strangers via Zelle or Venmo Friends & Family ‚Äî These offer no buyer protection. Use PayPal Goods & Services instead
  5. Watch for common scams:
    • Someone "accidentally" sends you money and asks you to send it back (original payment is fraudulent and will be reversed)
    • Requests from unknown numbers claiming to be friends or family in emergencies
    • Sellers asking for Zelle/Venmo payment instead of PayPal (avoiding buyer protection)
  6. Keep your app updated ‚Äî Security patches fix vulnerabilities; don't delay updates

Tax Implications of Money Transfers

An important note for anyone receiving payments through transfer apps:

Starting in 2023, payment apps (Venmo, PayPal, Cash App) must report to the IRS if you receive $600 or more in payments for goods and services in a calendar year. This is reported on Form 1099-K.

What this means for you:

  • Personal transfers are not affected ‚Äî Splitting rent, repaying friends, and gifting money are not taxable
  • Side hustle income IS affected ‚Äî If you sell goods or provide services through these apps, income over $600 is reported
  • Keep business and personal separate ‚Äî Use separate accounts or profiles for personal vs. business transactions
  • Track your deductions ‚Äî If you're earning side hustle income, track expenses to offset your tax liability. Our tax software guide can help

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest way to send money to someone?

For domestic transfers within the US, Zelle is the cheapest ‚Äî completely free with instant delivery. For international transfers, Wise typically offers the lowest total cost, using the real mid-market exchange rate with a transparent fee of 0.3-2%. Both are significantly cheaper than banks, which charge $25-50 per wire transfer plus unfavorable exchange rates.

Is Zelle safe to use for sending money?

Yes, Zelle is generally safe as it operates through your bank's security infrastructure. However, transfers are instant and irreversible ‚Äî once money is sent, you cannot cancel or reverse it. Only send money to people you know and trust. For purchases from strangers, use PayPal Goods & Services instead.

What is the best app for sending money internationally?

Wise (formerly TransferWise) is the best for most international transfers ‚Äî it uses the real mid-market exchange rate with transparent fees of 0.3-2%. For sending to developing countries where cash pickup is needed, Remitly is better. For large transfers over $10,000, OFX offers the best rates.

How long do money transfers take?

Zelle: minutes (instant). Venmo/Cash App: instant within app, 1-3 business days to bank (free), or instant to bank (1.5-1.75% fee). Wise international: 1-2 business days. PayPal: 1-3 business days to bank. Bank wires: 1-5 days domestic, 3-5 days international.

Do I have to pay taxes on money transfers?

Personal transfers (gifts, splitting bills, repaying friends) are generally not taxable. However, if you receive payments for goods or services totaling $600+ in a year through Venmo, PayPal, or Cash App, the platform must report it to the IRS via Form 1099-K. Keep personal and business transactions separate.

Can I send money without a bank account?

Yes. Cash App lets you sign up with just a phone number and fund transfers via debit card. PayPal lets you maintain a balance for sending. Western Union and MoneyGram accept cash at physical locations. Many apps also accept funding from prepaid debit cards.

What are hidden fees in money transfer apps?

The biggest hidden fee is the exchange rate markup on international transfers. Many services advertise "no fees" but build 2-5% into the exchange rate. Other hidden fees include credit card funding (2.5-3%), instant transfer fees (1.5-1.75%), inactivity fees, and receiving fees charged to the recipient. Always compare the total cost including the exchange rate.
