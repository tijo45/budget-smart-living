How to Do a Balance Transfer: Step-by-Step

  1. Check your credit score â€” You'll need 670+ for the best offers. Check for free at Credit Karma or your bank's app
  2. Calculate your payoff timeline â€” Divide your total debt by the intro period months. Can you afford those monthly payments? If your $9,000 balance needs to be paid in 18 months, that's $500/month
  3. Apply for the right card â€” Choose based on the comparison table above. Apply at a different bank than your current card issuer
  4. Request the balance transfer â€” Most issuers let you initiate this during the application or within 60-120 days of opening the account. You'll provide your old card's account number and the amount to transfer
  5. Keep paying the old card â€” It takes 5-14 days for transfers to process. Keep making minimum payments on the old card until you confirm the transfer is complete
  6. Set up autopay on the new card â€” At least the minimum payment; ideally the full payoff amount divided by your remaining intro months
  7. Don't add new debt â€” The whole point is to eliminate debt, not shift it around. Cut spending and put every extra dollar toward the balance

Balance Transfer vs. Personal Loan: Which Is Better?

Both can help you pay off high-interest credit card debt, but they work differently:

Factor Balance Transfer Card Personal Loan
Interest rate 0% for 12-21 months 5.99-35.99% fixed
Fees 3-5% transfer fee 0-8% origination fee
Payoff timeline 12-21 months (intro period) 2-7 years
Best for Debt under $10K you can pay off in 21 months Large debt ($10K+) needing longer payoff
Structure Flexible minimum payments Fixed monthly payments
Discipline needed High (no forced payoff schedule) Lower (fixed payments ensure payoff)

Rule of thumb: If you can pay off your debt within 18-21 months, a balance transfer card saves the most money (0% vs. 6-10% on a personal loan). If you need 3+ years, a personal loan's fixed structure prevents the "interest trap" when the intro period ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a balance transfer credit card?

A balance transfer credit card lets you move existing high-interest credit card debt to a new card with a 0% introductory APR period, typically lasting 12-21 months. During this period, 100% of your payments go toward the principal balance instead of interest, helping you pay off debt significantly faster. Most cards charge a balance transfer fee of 3-5%.

How much can I save with a balance transfer card?

On $5,000 of credit card debt at 22% APR, you'd pay roughly $1,100 in interest over 18 months. A balance transfer with a 3% fee ($150) saves you about $950. On $10,000 at 25% APR, savings can exceed $2,200 over a 21-month intro period.

What credit score do I need for a balance transfer card?

Most 0% APR balance transfer cards require good to excellent credit (FICO 670+). The longest intro periods (18-21 months) typically need 700+. Check for pre-qualification offers first â€” they use a soft pull that won't affect your score.

Can I transfer a balance between cards at the same bank?

Generally, no. Most issuers don't allow transfers between their own cards. You need to apply for a card from a different bank than your current high-interest card.

What happens when the 0% APR period ends?

The card's regular APR (typically 18-29%) kicks in on any remaining balance. Create a payoff plan to eliminate the balance before the intro period expires. Divide your total balance by the number of intro months to calculate your monthly target payment.

Is a balance transfer bad for my credit score?

Short-term: a small dip from the hard inquiry and lower average account age. Long-term: likely positive. The new credit line lowers your credit utilization ratio, and paying down debt improves your score significantly within 3-6 months.

Are there balance transfer cards with no transfer fee?

Yes, but they're rare and usually come with shorter 0% periods (12-15 months). For balances under $3,000, a no-fee card may save more. For larger balances, a longer 0% period with a 3% fee typically saves more in interest overall.
