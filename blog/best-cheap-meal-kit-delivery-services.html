Are Meal Kits Actually Cheaper Than Grocery Shopping?

Let's be honest: most meal kits are NOT cheaper than grocery shopping. Here's the real math:

  • Average grocery cost per serving (home-cooked): $2.50-4.00
  • Average meal kit cost per serving: $5.00-9.00
  • Average restaurant/takeout cost per serving: $12-18

Meal kits sit in the middle. They're cheaper than eating out but more expensive than grocery shopping from scratch. However, there are scenarios where meal kits can save you money:

  1. You currently eat out 3+ times per week ‚Äî switching to a meal kit saves $20-50/week
  2. You waste a lot of groceries ‚Äî pre-portioned ingredients eliminate food waste (Americans waste ~30% of food purchased)
  3. Your time has real value ‚Äî skipping meal planning and grocery shopping saves 3-5 hours/week
  4. You use introductory offers strategically ‚Äî rotating between services during promotional periods

Pro Tips to Save Even More on Meal Kits

  1. Stack introductory offers: Sign up for EveryPlate, use the 50% off deal, then switch to Dinnerly's intro offer. Rotate between services.
  2. Choose larger serving plans: The per-serving price drops 10-20% when you order 4 servings vs. 2 servings.
  3. Skip weeks strategically: All major services let you skip weeks without canceling. Use meal kits for busy weeks only.
  4. Combine with grocery savings strategies: Use meal kits for 3 dinners/week and cook budget meals from scratch for the rest.
  5. Check for cashback: Sites like Rakuten and TopCashback often offer 10-20% cashback on meal kit signups. Pair this with introductory discounts for maximum savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest meal kit delivery service?

EveryPlate is the cheapest traditional meal kit at $4.99 per serving. If you're willing to buy your own groceries, eMeals offers meal planning for just $5/month, bringing your total per-serving cost to $3-5 at grocery store prices.

Are meal kits worth it on a tight budget?

Meal kits are worth it if you currently spend a lot on takeout or waste groceries. They're typically $5-9 per serving vs. $12-18 for restaurants. However, cooking from scratch with groceries ($2.50-4/serving) is still the cheapest option.

Can I cancel a meal kit subscription anytime?

Yes, all major meal kit services (EveryPlate, Dinnerly, HelloFresh, Home Chef, Blue Apron, Gobble) allow you to cancel anytime with no penalties. You can also skip weeks without canceling. Just make sure to cancel before the weekly order cutoff (usually 5 days before delivery).
