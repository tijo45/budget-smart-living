What Does Renters Insurance Actually Cover?

Before comparing prices, make sure you understand the three pillars of renters insurance coverage:

1. Personal Property Coverage (Your Stuff)

This covers the cost to repair or replace your belongings if they're damaged or stolen due to covered events (fire, theft, vandalism, certain water damage, windstorms). The key terms to know:

  • Replacement cost ‚Äî Pays to buy a new, equivalent item at today's prices. A 3-year-old TV that cost $500 would be replaced with a comparable new TV for $500. Always choose replacement cost.
  • Actual cash value (ACV) ‚Äî Pays the depreciated value. That same 3-year-old $500 TV might only pay out $200 after depreciation. Avoid ACV policies unless you need the absolute lowest premium.

2. Liability Coverage (Lawsuits & Injuries)

If someone is injured in your rental, or if you accidentally damage someone else's property, liability coverage pays their medical bills and your legal defense costs. This is arguably the most valuable part of renters insurance.

Real scenario: A guest trips on your rug, breaks their wrist, and racks up $15,000 in medical bills. Without liability coverage, you're personally responsible. With $100,000 in liability, your insurance handles it.

3. Additional Living Expenses (ALE)

If your rental becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event (fire, severe water damage), ALE pays for temporary housing, food, and other necessary expenses while your unit is repaired. This can cover hotel stays, restaurant meals, and even increased commute costs for weeks or months.

How to Calculate the Right Coverage Amount

Don't guess how much coverage you need ‚Äî calculate it. Here's a quick home inventory method:

Walk through your apartment and estimate the replacement cost of everything in each room:

Bathroom Toiletries, towels, grooming tools $200‚Äì$500
Total $10,000‚Äì$30,000+

Step 2: Add 10-20% Buffer

People consistently underestimate how much they own. If your inventory totals $20,000, get $24,000-$25,000 in coverage. The premium difference between $20K and $25K coverage is usually less than $1/month.

Step 3: Check High-Value Item Limits

Standard policies cap payouts on certain categories:

  • Jewelry: Usually capped at $1,000‚Äì$1,500 per item
  • Electronics: May be capped at $2,500‚Äì$5,000 total
  • Musical instruments: Often capped at $2,000
  • Bicycles: Typically capped at $1,000

If you own items exceeding these limits, add a "scheduled personal property" rider. This costs $1-5/month per item but provides full coverage with no deductible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does renters insurance cost per month?

The average renters insurance policy costs $15-20 per month ($180-240 per year). However, budget-friendly providers like Lemonade, Toggle, and GEICO offer policies starting as low as $5-8 per month depending on your location, coverage amount, and deductible. Young renters with minimal belongings in low-risk areas can typically find policies under $10/month.

What does renters insurance actually cover?

Renters insurance covers three main areas: (1) Personal property ‚Äî your belongings (furniture, electronics, clothing) if damaged or stolen; (2) Liability protection ‚Äî covers legal and medical costs if someone is injured in your rental; (3) Additional living expenses (ALE) ‚Äî covers hotel, food, and other costs if your rental becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event like fire or water damage.

Is renters insurance required by law?

No state requires renters insurance by law. However, many landlords require it as a condition of your lease. Even if your landlord doesn't require it, it's strongly recommended ‚Äî your landlord's insurance only covers the building structure, not your personal belongings.

Does renters insurance cover roommates?

Generally, no. Standard renters insurance policies only cover the named policyholder and immediate family members living in the same household. Roommates typically need their own separate policies.

What is NOT covered by renters insurance?

Standard renters insurance typically does NOT cover: flood damage (requires separate flood insurance), earthquake damage (requires a separate rider), pest infestations, your car or items inside it (covered by auto insurance), intentional damage, normal wear and tear, or high-value items above policy limits. Always read your policy's exclusions carefully.

How do I file a renters insurance claim?

To file a claim: (1) Document the damage immediately ‚Äî take photos and videos; (2) Contact your insurer via app, phone, or website; (3) File a police report if theft or vandalism occurred; (4) Create a detailed inventory of damaged or stolen items; (5) Keep receipts for temporary living expenses if displaced. Most straightforward claims are processed within 1-2 weeks.

Can I get renters insurance with bad credit?

Yes, though premiums may be higher. Lemonade and Toggle are generally more credit-friendly than traditional insurers. Some states (California, Hawaii, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Oregon) prohibit or limit credit score use in insurance pricing. Shopping around is the best way to find affordable coverage regardless of credit.
