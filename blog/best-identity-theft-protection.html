What Identity Theft Protection Actually Does

Before diving into reviews, here's what these services monitor ‚Äî and what you can't easily do yourself:

üîí Identity Theft Protection Features Explained
  • Credit monitoring (1 or 3 bureau): Watches for new accounts, hard inquiries, address changes, and balance fluctuations on your credit reports
  • Dark web monitoring: Scans criminal marketplaces for your SSN, email addresses, passwords, bank account numbers, and medical ID
  • Public records monitoring: Tracks court records, sex offender registries, and change-of-address filings for your name/SSN
  • Social media monitoring: Watches for account takeover and suspicious activity on your social profiles
  • Financial account monitoring: Tracks bank accounts, credit cards, and investment accounts for unauthorized transactions
  • Identity theft insurance: Reimburses costs like legal fees, lost wages, and fraudulent charges ($1M-$5M coverage)
  • Recovery specialists: Dedicated agents who handle the entire restoration process if your identity is stolen

Frequently Asked Questions

Is identity theft protection worth paying for?

For most people, yes. 1.4 million Americans experienced identity theft in 2023. Victims spend an average of $1,400 and 200+ hours resolving it. Paid services provide 24/7 monitoring, dark web scanning, and $1M-$5M insurance for $7-30/month ‚Äî that's solid protection at a low cost. If you're very diligent about credit freezes and monitoring, free tools may be sufficient, but paid services add convenience, speed, and insurance.

What is the best free identity theft protection?

Combine Credit Karma (free 2-bureau monitoring), credit freezes at all 3 bureaus (free), Credit Sesame (free $50K insurance), Have I Been Pwned (free breach monitoring), and AnnualCreditReport.com (free weekly reports). Together, these provide solid baseline protection but lack dark web scanning, SSN monitoring, and the $1M+ insurance of paid services.

What is the difference between credit monitoring and identity theft protection?

Credit monitoring only watches your credit reports. Identity theft protection includes credit monitoring PLUS dark web scanning, SSN monitoring, court records, social media monitoring, bank account monitoring, recovery specialists, and $1M-$5M insurance. Credit monitoring is one piece; identity theft protection is the full puzzle.

Should I freeze my credit instead of paying for protection?

Do both. A credit freeze (free at all 3 bureaus) prevents new account fraud but doesn't protect against tax fraud, medical identity theft, existing account takeover, or criminal identity theft. Paid services monitor these additional vectors and provide insurance. Freeze your credit as a free first step, then add paid monitoring for comprehensive protection.

How much does identity theft protection cost?

$7-35/month for individuals. Zander is the cheapest at $7/month. Identity Guard starts at $8/month. Aura offers the best value at $12/month with VPN, password manager, and antivirus included. Family plans run $12.50-37/month. Annual billing saves 20-40% vs. monthly.

What should I do if my identity is stolen?

Act immediately: (1) Contact your ID protection provider, (2) Place fraud alerts at all 3 bureaus, (3) Freeze your credit, (4) Report to IdentityTheft.gov, (5) File a police report, (6) Dispute fraudulent charges with banks, (7) Change all passwords. With a paid service, a dedicated specialist handles most of these steps for you.

Can identity theft protection prevent identity theft?

No service can prevent identity theft entirely ‚Äî data breaches happen at companies holding your information, which is out of your control. What identity theft protection does is detect theft quickly (often within hours vs. months without monitoring), alert you immediately so you can act fast, minimize damage by catching fraud early, and provide insurance and recovery support to fix the damage. The faster you detect theft, the less damage it causes and the easier it is to resolve.
