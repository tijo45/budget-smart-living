FTC Compliance Statement

In accordance with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines, this page discloses our affiliate relationships and how we earn revenue to support this website.

How We Make Money

Budget Smart Living is a free resource dedicated to helping people improve their financial lives. To keep this site running and continue producing high-quality content, we earn revenue through the following methods:

1. Affiliate Marketing

Some of the links on our website are affiliate links. This means that if you click on the link and make a purchase or sign up for a service, we may receive a small commission at no additional cost to you. The price you pay is the same whether you use our affiliate link or go directly to the vendor's website.

2. Display Advertising

We display advertisements on our Site through Google AdSense and potentially other advertising networks. These ads generate revenue based on impressions and clicks. The advertisements you see may be personalized based on your browsing history and interests.

3. Sponsored Content

Occasionally, we may publish sponsored content or product reviews for which we receive compensation. Any sponsored content will always be clearly labeled as such.

  • Other affiliate networks: We participate in various affiliate networks including but not limited to ShareASale, CJ Affiliate, Impact, and Rakuten Advertising.

How Affiliate Links Work

When you click an affiliate link on our Site:

  1. You are redirected to the merchant's website
  2. A cookie is placed on your browser to track the referral
  3. If you make a qualifying purchase within the cookie's duration (usually 24 hours to 30 days, depending on the program), we receive a commission
  4. You pay the same price as if you had visited the merchant directly

Using our affiliate links is completely voluntary. If you prefer not to use them, you can navigate directly to any product or service we mention by typing the URL into your browser.
